# Brief: Learn 2 Live Legacy — Digital Platform for The Forgiveness Framework
Date: 2026-04-09
Status: Ready — build approved
Requested by: Jermaine Wong / Learn 2 Live Legacy

---

## Problem

Young people, educators, funders, and civic partners who encounter Learn 2 Live Legacy have no digital home to understand the Forgiveness Framework, connect with its philosophy, and take meaningful action — resulting in lost partnerships, missed funding opportunities, and communities that can't access or advocate for the programme.

---

## User

**Primary: Funders & Civic Partners**
Who: Arts Council officers, local authority commissioners, borough arts venues (e.g. Brixton House), trust/foundation programme managers
Current behaviour: Receiving the PDF proposal over email, visiting no website, making decisions on the basis of a document alone
Frequency: One-off discovery, high-stakes decision

**Secondary: Educators & Schools**
Who: Deputy headteachers, pastoral leads, PSHE coordinators — predominantly London secondary schools and further education colleges
Current behaviour: Hearing about the programme via word-of-mouth or the Kiyan Prince Foundation; no self-serve information available
Frequency: Annual curriculum planning cycles

**Tertiary: Young People & Families**
Who: 14–21 year olds in South London boroughs, parents, youth workers
Current behaviour: May encounter Learn 2 Live Legacy through school or community events; no awareness of the wider framework
Frequency: Event-led, episodic

---

## Success Criteria

Observable outcomes that define success — not features delivered:

- [ ] A funder or partner can understand the Forgiveness Framework, its evidence base, and its delivery model in under 3 minutes without needing the PDF
- [ ] An educator can find enough information to make a first contact enquiry without prior knowledge of the organisation
- [ ] A visitor can understand Jermaine Wong's personal story and its relationship to the programme's philosophy within the first scroll
- [ ] A young person shown the site at a school event recognises themselves in the content and feels the space is for them
- [ ] The site can be used as a leave-behind URL in partnership meetings, replacing the PDF as the primary first-impression asset
- [ ] Funders can access the financial model, pilot evidence, and scalability plan without asking for additional documents

---

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Single marketing/information website for Learn 2 Live Legacy and The Forgiveness Framework
- Sections covering: the Framework overview, programme structure (4 phases), Our Son (the artistic anchor and its integration with the framework — no separate website), philosophical foundation, evidence from previous delivery, partnerships, financial framework, and future scalability
- Statistics and impact figures drawn directly from the Framework proposal document (embedded throughout, not in a separate section)
- A clear and prominent call-to-action for partnership/funding enquiries, including a contact form
- Jermaine Wong's story written in first person — tone, cadence, and rhythm must match the Framework document
- Downloadable PDF of The Forgiveness Framework Proposal as a supporting resource
- Mobile-responsive design (primary viewing context likely mobile for secondary audiences)
- Existing photography and video assets from prior Our Son performances and Live events to be used throughout
- Domain procurement — no existing domain or hosting, needs to be sourced as part of this project
- Our Son performance information integrated into the main site (ticketing/booking out of scope for Phase 1)

**Out of scope:**
- A member login or participant portal
- A separate Our Son website or booking system
- A separate youth-facing content section (may be Phase 2)
- New photography or video production — existing assets only
- Full CMS / blog infrastructure (unless needed for programme updates)
- Copy writing beyond structural framework — first-person copy to be supplied or approved by Jermaine Wong

---

## CRISP Priority

**Primary dimension: C (Contextual)** — Audiences who land on this site have no prior context for the organisation or programme. The primary design challenge is orientation: who is this, what do they do, why does it matter, and what should I do next? Every section must answer a specific question the audience is carrying when they arrive.

**Secondary dimension: S (Seamless)** — Funders and educators operate in high-friction environments. The site must let them find exactly what they need (evidence, costs, contact) without friction, dead ends, or the need to email for documents they expect to find online.
